What Is the Aviator Game?

Aviator is a multiplier game: a plane lifts off and your potential payout grows the longer it stays on screen. Rounds stop at random—the moment the plane goes, any bet you haven't banked is gone. You're trying to lock in a win before that happens.

  • Launched: 2019. 97% RTP, stakes from $0.10 to $100. Runs on desktop, iOS, Android, tablets.
  • Dual bets: Run two positions in one round—handy for mixing safe and risky plays.
  • Provably Fair: Every result can be checked via server and client seeds; no black box.
  • Live feed: You see everyone's stakes and cash-outs as they happen.
How It Works

How Aviator Game Works

Screen shows a plane on a graph. You stake, the round starts, and the multiplier climbs with the plane. Whatever multiplier you lock in is your payout. If the plane disappears before you bank, that stake is lost.

  • When the round ends is decided by an RNG—nobody can know in advance.
  • Plenty of players chase bigger multipliers; others watch the room and manage their stack.
  • Provably Fair uses a server seed plus the first three players' client seeds to produce the outcome.
  • In the game menu you can check the hashed server seed and your client seed to confirm the result.

Tips

Aviator Tips and Responsible Gambling

Cap your spend, keep a separate bankroll, and never chase what you've lost. Only play with money you can afford to lose.

  • Bigger stakes, earlier cash-out: Bank at lower multipliers for steadier, smaller wins.
  • Small stakes, wait for a spike: Less risk per round; when the multiplier runs high, the payout can be large.
  • Two positions: One conservative and one aggressive in the same round spreads the risk.
  • Stay in control: Use daily, weekly, or monthly limits and take breaks. "Predictors" and "Aviator hacks" are scams and don't work.
